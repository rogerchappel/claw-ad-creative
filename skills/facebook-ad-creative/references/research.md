# Research

## Source Mix

Use a mix of:

- direct competitors,
- adjacent competitors,
- aspirational advertisers,
- marketplaces,
- consultants,
- data products,
- industry events,
- premium tools with similar buying psychology.

Do not limit research to exact-match competitors. The best patterns often come
from adjacent markets with the same urgency, trust problem, or buyer status.

## Capture Fields

For each ad, capture:

- advertiser,
- URL or source reference,
- observed date,
- active dates if visible,
- format,
- hook,
- opening frame,
- core claim,
- offer,
- proof,
- CTA,
- landing page,
- visual style,
- likely funnel stage,
- why it might be working,
- what not to copy.

## Pattern Extraction

Look for:

- repeated hooks across advertisers,
- ad formats that appear often,
- long-running ads,
- proof mechanisms,
- urgency devices,
- buyer fears,
- objections being answered,
- offer framing,
- creator/UGC patterns,
- before/after structure,
- comparison structure.

## Research Rules

- Do not scrape private data.
- Do not copy competitor creative or proprietary assets.
- Do not infer exact performance from visibility alone.
- Say "appears to be working" only when the evidence supports it, such as long
  active duration, repeated variants, or recurring pattern across advertisers.
- Keep source references so the user can audit the inspiration.
