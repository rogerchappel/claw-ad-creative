# Creative Strategy

## Build Angles From Buyer Psychology

Strong Meta ads usually make one sharp promise to one buyer in one moment.

Useful angle families:

- pain-to-relief,
- status gain,
- speed,
- certainty,
- missed opportunity,
- social proof,
- expert authority,
- before/after,
- comparison,
- objection handling,
- product demo,
- deadline urgency,
- cost of doing nothing.

## Concept Template

For each concept, define:

- name,
- audience,
- funnel stage,
- buyer emotion,
- hook,
- proof,
- offer,
- visual,
- CTA,
- success metric,
- risk or compliance concern.

## Meta-Native Creative Heuristics

- The first 1 to 2 seconds must earn attention.
- Use clear visual hierarchy; mobile users skim.
- One ad should sell one action.
- The visual should communicate before the caption is read.
- Proof beats adjectives.
- Specificity beats generic benefit language.
- Objection-handling ads often work well for warm audiences.
- Avoid beautiful but unclear creative.

## Copy Rules

Write copy in plain language. Avoid:

- inflated claims,
- fake scarcity,
- unverifiable performance promises,
- "revolutionary" filler,
- copying competitor phrasing,
- too many benefits in one ad.

Prefer:

- direct hooks,
- concrete outcomes,
- audience-specific vocabulary,
- proof,
- concise CTAs,
- a clear reason to act now.

## Batch Shape

A useful first batch is:

- 3 problem/solution ads,
- 2 proof or authority ads,
- 2 product/demo ads,
- 1 comparison ad,
- 1 urgency/event ad,
- 1 wildcard learning ad.
