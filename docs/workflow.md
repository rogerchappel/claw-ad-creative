# Workflow

## 1. Brief

Capture the job before researching:

- product,
- target customer,
- geographic market,
- offer,
- conversion event,
- landing page,
- exclusions,
- brand rules,
- assets already available,
- budget range,
- deadline.

## 2. Competitor Research

Collect 10 to 30 relevant ads. Include direct and adjacent competitors.

For each ad, record:

- advertiser,
- platform and placement clues,
- active dates when visible,
- format,
- hook,
- offer,
- proof,
- visual pattern,
- CTA,
- likely funnel stage,
- why it may be working.

Do not copy a competitor's ad. Extract the pattern and create original work.

## 3. Creative Strategy

Build 5 to 10 angles from the research:

- pain-to-relief,
- status gain,
- speed or convenience,
- fear of missing out,
- proof and authority,
- before/after,
- objection handling,
- demo or walkthrough,
- comparison,
- event/deadline urgency.

Every angle should include a buyer psychology note and a measurable hypothesis.

## 4. Asset Generation

Create prompt packs by format:

- square static,
- vertical story/reel,
- carousel,
- short video,
- thumbnail.

Generate multiple concepts before polishing. Keep source prompts, model names,
settings, generated files, and rejection notes.

## 5. Approval Pack

Before anything touches an ad account, produce:

- concept names,
- assets,
- copy variants,
- targeting hypothesis,
- objective,
- landing page,
- UTM plan,
- spend recommendation,
- risks,
- what requires approval.

## 6. Draft Creation

Only after approval:

- create draft or paused ads,
- attach assets,
- set naming conventions,
- add UTMs,
- verify links,
- leave publishing off.

## 7. Reporting

Report after a meaningful spend or time window. Include:

- what ran,
- spend,
- reach,
- impressions,
- CPM,
- CTR,
- CPC,
- conversion metrics,
- best creative,
- worst creative,
- likely reason,
- recommendation: scale, iterate, pause, or learn more.

## 8. Learning Loop

Turn results back into the next brief:

- winning hooks,
- losing hooks,
- format signals,
- audience signals,
- objection signals,
- next 3 tests.
