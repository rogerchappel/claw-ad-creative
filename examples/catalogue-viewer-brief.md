# Example Brief: Catalogue Viewer

Product: premium horse catalogue viewer for upcoming sales.

Audience:

- bloodstock agents,
- trainers,
- owners,
- syndicators,
- serious buyers building shortlists before a sale.

Core promise:

- inspect more lots faster,
- find shortlist-worthy horses earlier,
- unlock premium lot intelligence when it matters.

Creative angles:

- "Stop losing the good ones in a spreadsheet."
- "Your sale shortlist, upgraded."
- "See the lot details that change the conversation."
- "Black type, runner model, parade notes, and pose signals in one report."
- "Do the first pass in minutes, then spend your attention on the right horses."

Competitor research seeds:

- horse auction platforms,
- Magic Millions and Inglis sale-related advertisers,
- bloodstock agents,
- racing data products,
- premium sports analytics products,
- financial research tools with paid reports,
- property buyer-agent tools.

Offer hypotheses:

- free credits for first-time lot unlocks,
- Agent plan with monthly credits,
- Elite plan with higher monthly credits and discounted top-ups,
- sale-week shortlist report bundle.

Primary conversion:

- account signup,
- plan upgrade,
- credit top-up,
- generated lot report.

Brand constraints:

- premium but practical,
- no hype about guaranteed winners,
- no unsupported claims,
- keep visuals grounded in real sale and catalogue workflows.

3D device creative direction:

- Use a model-rendered 3D iPhone or iPad for the physical device angle.
- Do not create fake 3D device depth with deterministic code or perspective
  warping.
- Keep the device angle natural: the phone should lean toward the viewer with a
  thin metal side edge, realistic bevel, glass reflection, and contact shadow.
- Reject outputs with a thick black side slab, visible cutout plate, floating
  device, impossible shadow, or rewritten app UI.
- Use deterministic layout only for exact brand text, CTA, final crop, contact
  sheet, and export.
- Preferred scene styles:
  - sale-week desk with catalogue pages, notebook, pen, and paddock beyond,
  - stable-lane inspection with warm barn light,
  - breeder pedigree desk with mare and foal in the field,
  - navy-and-gold bloodstock agent desk with pedigree papers.
